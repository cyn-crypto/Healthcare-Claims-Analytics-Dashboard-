## 💡 Project Insights

### Northeast & West carry the highest cost burden
Average claim costs in the Northeast and West run ~25% above the Southwest — driven by higher facility rates, specialist utilization, and payer mix. This regional variation has direct implications for plan design and network strategy.

### A small patient cohort drives a disproportionate share of spend
The top 5% of patients by total cost account for roughly 40% of overall spend. These high-cost claimants are concentrated in hospital and ER settings and carry an average of 4+ chronic conditions — making them prime candidates for care management intervention.

### Chronic conditions are the strongest readmission predictor
30-day hospital readmission rates increase by approximately 2 percentage points for each additional chronic condition a patient carries. Patients with 5 or more conditions show readmission rates nearly double that of patients with none — a clear signal for post-discharge follow-up protocols.

### Denial rates flag payer-level operational risk
Two payers exceeded the 5% denial rate threshold. Self-Pay patients face the steepest financial exposure, absorbing ~55% of billed costs out-of-pocket compared to ~20% for Medicare beneficiaries — highlighting equity gaps in coverage adequacy.

### ER utilization varies significantly by region
The Southeast shows the highest ER visit rates relative to total claims, suggesting gaps in primary care access or care coordination that are redirecting patients to more expensive acute settings.
